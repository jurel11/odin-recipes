Adobo Baboy

Description

Adobong baboy is a traditional stew originating from the Philippines. This type of Filipino adobo is made with a combination of pork belly, garlic, soy sauce, vinegar, sugar, bay leaves, peppercorns, and salt. The pork is marinated in a combination of garlic and soy sauce, and it's then browned in a pan over high heat.

The rest of the ingredients are added to the pan (except the vinegar), and the dish is simmered for a few minutes before the vinegar, salt, and water or soy sauce are added to the pan. Once fully cooked, this pork stew is served hot with the thick sauce and white rice on the side.

Ingredients

  • Pork
  • Soy Sauce
  • Vinegar
  • Garlic
  • Sugar
  • Bay Leaves
  • Peppercorns

Steps

  1. Marinate the Pork: In a bowl, combine the pork, soy sauce, and half of the crushed garlic. Marinate for at least 30 minutes to 1 hour.
  2. Sauté: Heat oil in a pot over medium heat. Sauté the remaining garlic (and onion, if using) until fragrant.
  3. Brown the Pork: Add the marinated pork (including the marinade) to the pot. Cook until the pork is browned on all sides.
  4. Add Ingredients: Pour in the vinegar, water, bay leaves, peppercorns, and sugar (if using). Bring to a boil without stirring.
  5. Simmer: Lower the heat and let it simmer for about 40 minutes to 1 hour, or until the pork is tender and the sauce has reduced to your desired consistency.
  6. Season: Taste and adjust the seasoning with salt if needed.
  7. Serve: Serve hot with steamed rice.
